# Brief description

A hydraulic press is a machine press using a hydraulic cylinder to generate a compressive force. It uses the hydraulic equivalent of a mechanical lever, and was known as a Bramah press after the inventor, Joseph Bramah.

## Use / Function

- **Forging and Stamping:** Shaping large metal parts with steady, massive pressure.
- **Crushing:** Compressing scrap metal or extracting oils from seeds.
- **Molding:** Pressing powders into solid forms or shaping plastics.
- **Lifting:** Acting as a heavy-duty jack.
- **Scale:** From small lab presses to industrial giants exerting thousands of tons of force.

## Operating principle

The hydraulic press works based on Pascal's Principle: "Pressure exerted anywhere in a confined incompressible fluid is transmitted equally in all directions throughout the fluid."

1.  **System:** Two cylinders of different diameters (small 'plunger' and large 'ram') connected by a pipe filled with liquid.
2.  **Input Force:** A small force is applied to the small plunger.
3.  **Pressure:** This creates pressure in the fluid (Pressure = Force / Area).
4.  **Transmission:** The pressure is transmitted through the pipe to the large cylinder.
5.  **Output Force:** The same pressure acts on the large area of the ram, resulting in a much larger force (Force = Pressure x Area).

## How to create it

1.  **The Frame:** A massive, heavy-duty steel frame (usually H-frame) to contain the reaction forces.
2.  **Cylinders:** Two precision-bored cylinders (one small, one large).
3.  **The Ram:** A large piston that fits into the large cylinder.
4.  **The Pump:** A small piston (plunger) operated by a hand lever or motor to move fluid.
5.  **Seals:** Critical **[Leather](/en/materials/leather)** or rubber cup seals (U-seals) that expand under pressure to prevent leaks.
6.  **Valves:** One-way check valves to allow fluid to flow from the reservoir to the pump and then to the ram, and a release valve to return fluid to the reservoir.

## Materials needed

- **Frame/Cylinders:** **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)** or **[Cast Iron](/en/materials/cast-iron)**.
- **Working Fluid:** **[Water](/en/materials/water)** (early versions) or **[Vegetable Oil](/en/materials/vegetable-oil)** (prevents rust and lubricates).
- **Seals:** **[Leather](/en/materials/leather)** (treated) or synthetic rubber.
- **Piping:** High-pressure steel tubing.

## Variants and improvements

- **Manual Hydraulic Press:** Hand-pumped, common in workshops.
- **Power Press:** Uses an electric or steam-driven pump for continuous high-speed operation.
- **Intensifier:** A device to increase pressure even further for specialized tasks.

## Limits and risks

- **Fluid Leaks:** Even a pinhole leak under high pressure can be dangerous (hydraulic injection injury).
- **Seal Failure:** If seals fail, the press loses power immediately.
- **Bursting:** If the pressure exceeds the strength of the cylinders or pipes, they can explode.
- **Slow Speed:** Generally slower than mechanical presses.
