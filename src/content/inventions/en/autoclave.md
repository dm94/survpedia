# Brief description

An autoclave is a strong, heated container used for chemical reactions and other processes using high pressure and temperature. Its primary purpose is sterilization and curing materials.

## Use / Function

- **Sterilization**: Killing all forms of life, including spores, on medical and laboratory equipment.
- **Curing**: Processing composite materials and vulcanizing rubber.
- **Industrial Processing**: Facilitating chemical reactions that require high pressure and heat.
- **Scale**: From small tabletop units for dental clinics to massive industrial vessels.

## Operating principle

The autoclave works on the principle of using saturated steam under pressure to achieve temperatures above the normal boiling point of water.

1.  **Water Heating**: Water is heated to produce steam.
2.  **Pressure Increase**: As the steam is trapped in a sealed vessel, the pressure rises.
3.  **Temperature Elevation**: Increased pressure allows the steam to reach temperatures (typically 121°C or 134°C) that would be impossible at atmospheric pressure.
4.  **Penetration**: The pressurized steam penetrates deep into porous materials or narrow crevices, ensuring complete sterilization or uniform heating.

## How to create it

Building a safe autoclave requires high-level mechanical engineering and precision:

- **Pressure Vessel**: A thick-walled cylinder, usually made of **[Stainless Steel](/en/materials/stainless-steel)** or **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)**, capable of withstanding several atmospheres of pressure.
- **Sealing Mechanism**: A heavy door with a heat-resistant **[Natural Rubber](/en/materials/natural-rubber)** or silicone gasket to ensure an airtight seal.
- **Heating System**: Electric heaters or a steam generator.
- **Safety Valves**: Crucial components that release pressure if it exceeds safe limits to prevent explosion.
- **Technical level**: Advanced.

## Materials needed

- **Essential materials**:
  - **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)** or **[Stainless Steel](/en/materials/stainless-steel)**: For the main vessel.
  - **[Natural Rubber](/en/materials/natural-rubber)**: For gaskets and seals.
  - **[Water](/en/materials/water)**: To generate steam.
- **Tools**:
  - **[Arc Welder](/en/inventions/arc-welder)**: To assemble the pressure vessel.
  - **[Lathe](/en/inventions/lathe)**: For machining precise sealing surfaces.
- **Possible substitutes**:
  - **Pressure Cooker**: A domestic version for small-scale sterilization.

## Variants and improvements

- **Gravity Displacement**: Steam enters and pushes out the denser air.
- **Pre-vacuum**: Uses a vacuum pump to remove air before steam entry, ensuring better penetration.
- **Industrial Autoclaves**: Massive units used for vulcanizing tires or curing aircraft parts.

## Limits and risks

- **Explosion Risk**: If the pressure vessel fails or the safety valve is blocked, it can explode with devastating force.
- **Burn Hazard**: High-temperature steam and hot surfaces can cause severe burns.
- **Material Degradation**: Some materials (like certain plastics) cannot withstand the high heat and pressure.
- **Incomplete Sterilization**: If air is trapped inside, the steam may not reach all surfaces.
