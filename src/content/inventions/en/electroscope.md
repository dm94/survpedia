# Brief description

An electroscope is an early scientific instrument used to detect the presence and magnitude of electric charge on a body. It was one of the first tools used to explore the laws of electrostatics.

## Use / Function

- **Charge Detection:** Identifying if an object carries an electric charge.
- **Charge Type:** Determining the polarity (positive or negative) of a charge when compared to a known reference.
- **Radiation Detection:** Measuring ionizing radiation (as it causes the charge to leak away).
- **Education:** Demonstrating basic principles of electricity.

## Operating principle

The electroscope operates on the principle of **electrostatic repulsion**:

1.  When a charged object touches or comes near the top metal terminal, some of the charge is transferred to the internal metal components.
2.  Two thin, flexible metal leaves (usually gold or silver) at the bottom of the device become similarly charged.
3.  Because like charges repel each other, the leaves push apart. The distance they move is proportional to the amount of charge present.

## How to create it

1.  **Preparation:** Clean a clear **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** jar and ensure it is completely dry.
2.  **The Terminal:** Create a metal ball or disc to act as the external terminal.
3.  **The Rod:** Attach a thick **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)** wire (copper or brass) to the terminal.
4.  **Insulation:** Pass the rod through an insulating stopper (cork, rubber, or plastic) that fits the jar.
5.  **The Leaves:** At the bottom of the rod inside the jar, attach two extremely thin strips of **[Gold](/en/materials/gold)** leaf or **[Silver](/en/materials/silver)** foil. They should be hung so they touch when uncharged.
6.  **Assembly:** Seal the jar with the stopper, ensuring the leaves are hanging freely in the center without touching the glass walls.

## Materials needed

- **Conductors:** **[Gold](/en/materials/gold)** leaf, **[Silver](/en/materials/silver)** foil, or **[Aluminum](/en/materials/aluminum)** foil.
- **Support:** **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)** wire (copper or brass).
- **Container:** Clear **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** jar.
- **Insulator:** Cork or plastic stopper.

## Variants and improvements

- **Pith-ball Electroscope:** Uses two small balls of lightweight pith (plant material) suspended by silk threads.
- **Faraday Cage:** Encasing the electroscope in a wire mesh to protect it from external electric fields.
- **Quadrant Electrometer:** A more sophisticated version that can provide quantitative measurements of potential difference.

## Limits and risks

- **Humidity:** Water vapor in the air can bleed away the charge, making the device fail in damp conditions.
- **Sensitivity:** Very small charges may not be strong enough to move the leaves.
- **Fragility:** The metal leaves are extremely delicate and can be easily torn or damaged by sudden movements.
