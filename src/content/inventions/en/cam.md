# Brief description

A cam is a rotating or sliding piece in a mechanical linkage used especially in transforming rotary motion into linear motion or vice versa. It is often a wheel with an irregular shape or an offset center.

## Use / Function

- **Timing Control:** Opening and closing valves in engines at precise moments.
- **Automation:** Programming a sequence of movements in a machine (like a loom or an automaton).
- **Force Transformation:** Converting the continuous rotation of a water wheel into the rhythmic striking of a trip hammer or the pumping of [Bellows](/en/inventions/bellows).

## Operating principle

A cam works through its profile (shape). As the cam rotates, a "follower" (a rod or lever) stays in contact with the cam's edge. The irregular shape of the cam pushes the follower up and down. The specific shape of the cam determines the timing, speed, and distance of the follower's movement.

## How to create it

1.  **Design the Profile:** Determine the required motion and draw the shape on your material. An egg shape (eccentric) is the simplest.
2.  **Cut and Shape:** Cut the cam from [Wood](/en/materials/wood) or [Metal](/en/materials/metal). The surface must be very smooth to reduce friction and wear.
3.  **Mounting:** Fix the cam onto a rotating [Axle](/en/inventions/axle).
4.  **The Follower:** Position a lever or rod so that one end rests on the edge of the cam. Use a spring or gravity to ensure the follower always stays in contact with the cam.

## Materials needed

- **Cam Body:** Hard [Wood](/en/materials/wood) (like oak), [Iron](/en/materials/iron), or [Steel](/en/materials/steel) for durability.
- **Follower:** [Metal](/en/materials/metal) is preferred to resist wear.
- **Lubricant:** [Animal Fat](/en/materials/animal-fat) or oil to keep the contact point from wearing down.

## Variants and improvements

- **Plate Cam:** A flat disk with a shaped edge.
- **Cylindrical Cam:** A groove cut into a cylinder.
- **Eccentric:** A simple circular cam mounted off-center.

## Limits and risks

- **Wear:** The constant friction between the cam and the follower causes significant wear. Hardened surfaces are essential for longevity.
- **Speed Limits:** At very high speeds, the follower may "float" (lose contact) if the return spring is too weak.
