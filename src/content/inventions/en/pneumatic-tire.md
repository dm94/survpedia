# Brief description

A pneumatic tire is a flexible, air-filled ring that surrounds a wheel's rim. It provides traction, absorbs shocks from uneven terrain, and protects the wheel from wear. It revolutionized transportation by making vehicles faster, more efficient, and more comfortable.

## Use / Function

- **Transportation**: Essential for bicycles, cars, trucks, and aircraft.
- **Shock Absorption**: Acts as a primary suspension component by cushioning impacts.
- **Traction**: Provides a high-friction interface between the vehicle and the road.
- **Scale**: From small bicycle tires to massive mining truck tires.

## Operating principle

The tire works on the principle of a pressurized container:
1. **Containment**: A strong, flexible casing (made of rubber and fabric) holds compressed air.
2. **Deformation**: The air pressure allows the tire to deform slightly when it hits a bump, spreading the force over a larger area.
3. **Recovery**: The compressed air pushes back, returning the tire to its shape and providing a "spring" effect.
4. **Friction**: The rubber tread provides grip through molecular adhesion and mechanical interlocking with the road surface.

## How to create it

Creating a durable pneumatic tire requires advanced materials and processes:
1. **Casing Construction**: Layers of **[Fabric](/en/materials/fabric)** (traditionally canvas or nylon) are coated with rubber to form the "carcass" or "plies."
2. **Bead Formation**: Steel **[Wire](/en/materials/wire)** is used to create "beads" that hold the tire firmly against the wheel rim.
3. **Tread Application**: A thicker layer of tough, abrasion-resistant rubber is applied to the outer circumference.
4. **Vulcanization**: The entire assembly is placed in a mold and heated with sulfur (**[Vulcanization](/en/knowledge/vulcanization)**) to chemically bond the layers and give the rubber its final properties.
5. **Inflation**: The tire is mounted on a rim and filled with compressed air via a **[Valve](/en/inventions/valve)**.

## Materials needed

- **Elastic Base**: **[Natural Rubber](/en/materials/natural-rubber)** (or synthetic rubber).
- **Reinforcement**: **[Fabric](/en/materials/fabric)** (cotton, silk, or synthetic fibers).
- **Structural Integrity**: **[Wire](/en/materials/wire)** (steel) for the beads.
- **Chemicals**: Sulfur for vulcanization.
- **Inflation**: Compressed air.

## Variants and improvements

- **Solid Rubber Tire**: Durable but lacks the shock absorption of pneumatic tires.
- **Inner Tube System**: A separate air-tight tube inside a protective outer casing (standard for many years).
- **Tubeless Tire**: The tire itself forms an air-tight seal with the rim; more resistant to blowouts.
- **Radial vs. Bias-ply**: Different ways of arranging the fabric layers for better handling or durability.

## Limits and risks

- **Punctures**: Sharp objects can cause the air to leak, rendering the tire flat.
- **Blowouts**: Sudden loss of pressure at high speeds can lead to loss of vehicle control.
- **Degradation**: Rubber perishes over time when exposed to UV light and ozone.
- **Pressure Sensitivity**: Over-inflated or under-inflated tires have reduced performance and higher wear.
