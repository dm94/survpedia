# Brief description

A loom is a device used to weave cloth and tapestry. The basic purpose of any loom is to hold the warp threads under tension to facilitate the interweaving of the weft threads. The precise shape of the loom and its mechanics may vary, but the basic function is the same.

## Use / Function

Its practical purpose:
- Production of textiles for clothing, blankets, and storage sacks.
- Creation of strong fabrics for sails, tents, and industrial uses.
- Weaving of decorative tapestries and rugs.

## Operating principle

Weaving is the process of interlacing two sets of threads: the **warp** (longitudinal threads held in tension) and the **weft** (transverse threads). The loom holds the warp threads parallel. A mechanism (like a heddle) lifts or lowers specific warp threads to create an opening called a **shed**. The weft thread is passed through the shed using a **shuttle**. Then, a **reed** or comb beats the new weft thread against the previous ones to create a tight fabric.

## How to create it

- **Minimum functional version**: A **Backstrap Loom**, where the warp is tied between a stationary object and the weaver's waist.
- **Essential**: A frame to hold tension, a way to separate threads (heddles), and a shuttle to carry the weft.
- **Technical level**: Intermediate to High (depending on the complexity of the shedding mechanism).

## Materials needed

- **Essential materials**: **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** (for the frame and shuttle), and textile fibers like **[Cotton](/en/materials/cotton)**, **[Wool](/en/materials/wool)**, or **[Linen](/en/materials/linen)**.
- **Tools**: **[Saw](/en/inventions/saw)**, **[Chisel](/en/inventions/chisel)**, **[Knife](/en/inventions/knife)**.

## Variants and improvements

- **Backstrap Loom**: Portable and simple, uses the weaver's body for tension.
- **Warp-weighted Loom**: Vertical loom where weights hold the warp threads down.
- **Floor Loom**: Large, stationary horizontal loom operated by foot pedals (treadles) to move the heddles.
- **Flying Shuttle**: An improvement that allows the shuttle to be thrown across the loom quickly, increasing weaving speed.
- **Power Loom**: A mechanized version driven by steam or water power.

## Limits and risks

- **Complexity**: Setting up the warp (warping) is a time-consuming and complex process.
- **Mechanical Wear**: Moving parts, especially in large looms, require maintenance and lubrication.
- **Ergonomics**: Long hours of weaving can lead to repetitive strain injuries.
