# Brief description

A rocket stove is an efficient and hot burning stove using small-diameter wood fuel. It achieves high combustion efficiency by ensuring a good air draft and a well-insulated combustion chamber.

## Use / Function

- **Primary use**: Efficient cooking with minimal fuel.
- **Secondary uses**: Space heating (Rocket Mass Heaters), water heating.
- **Scale**: Small portable units to large built-in home heaters.

## Operating principle

**The L-shaped combustion chamber**:

1.  **Air Intake**: Fuel is fed into the bottom of an L-shaped tube. Air enters below the fuel.
2.  **Combustion**: Wood burns at the corner of the L.
3.  **Chimney Effect**: The vertical part of the L acts as an internal chimney, creating a strong draft that pulls in air and keeps the fire hot.
4.  **Insulation**: The chamber is heavily insulated so that the fire burns as hot as possible, ensuring near-complete combustion of gases and smoke.

## How to create it

### 1. The Chamber

- Create an L-shaped tube using **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)** pipes or **[Bricks](/en/materials/brick)**.
- The horizontal part is the fuel feed; the vertical part is the combustion chimney.

### 2. Insulation

- Place the L-chamber inside a larger container (like a 5-gallon bucket or a brick structure).
- Fill the gap between the chamber and the outer container with an insulating material like **[Perlite](/en/materials/perlite)**, **[Vermiculite](/en/materials/vermiculite)**, or wood ash.

### 3. Pot Support

- Place a pot support at the top of the vertical chimney, ensuring there is enough gap for the hot gases to escape around the bottom of the pot.

## Materials needed

- **Internal Chamber**: **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)** pipe, fire **[Bricks](/en/materials/brick)**, or **[Clay](/en/materials/clay)**.
- **Insulation**: Wood ash, **[Perlite](/en/materials/perlite)**, or dry **[Stone](/en/materials/stone)**.
- **Outer Shell**: **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)** cans, drums, or **[Masonry](/en/inventions/masonry)**.

## Variants and improvements

- **Rocket Mass Heater**: The exhaust is piped through a large thermal mass (like a cob bench) to store and slowly release heat.
- **Institutional Rocket Stove**: Large-scale versions for schools or hospitals.

## Limits and risks

- **Fuel Preparation**: Requires fuel to be broken down into small sticks.
- **Carbon Monoxide**: Like any stove, it must be used in a well-ventilated area or vented outdoors.
- **Heat**: The vertical chimney and the area around it get extremely hot.
