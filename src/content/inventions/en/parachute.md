# Brief description

A parachute is a device used to slow the motion of an object through an atmosphere by creating drag. It is primarily used to allow a safe descent from high altitudes for people, equipment, or vehicles.

## Use / Function

- **Safe Descent:** Allowing humans or cargo to land safely after being dropped from an aircraft or high point.
- **Braking:** Slowing down fast-moving vehicles (like land speed record cars or returning spacecraft) during landing.
- **Stability:** Providing stabilization for dropped objects.
- **Scale:** Individual (human) to Industrial (cargo/spacecraft).

## Operating principle

The parachute works by increasing the surface area of an object, thereby increasing air resistance (drag).
1.  **Deployment:** The folded parachute is released and opens up due to air pressure.
2.  **Drag Generation:** The large canopy captures air, creating a high-pressure zone underneath it.
3.  **Terminal Velocity Reduction:** The drag force opposes the force of gravity, slowing the descent until a manageable "terminal velocity" is reached.

## How to create it

### Basic Round Parachute (Intermediate)

1.  **Canopy:** A large, circular or polygonal sheet made of strong, lightweight, and low-porosity fabric.
2.  **Shroud Lines:** Multiple **[Rope](/en/materials/rope)** or cords of equal length attached to the perimeter of the canopy.
3.  **Apex Vent:** A small hole at the very center of the canopy to allow some air to escape, which prevents the parachute from oscillating (swinging) wildly.
4.  **Harness:** A system of straps that connects the shroud lines to the load or person.
5.  **Packing:** The canopy must be carefully folded to ensure it opens reliably without tangling the lines.

## Materials needed

- **Canopy:** **[Silk](/en/materials/silk)** (traditional, best strength-to-weight), **[Linen](/en/materials/linen)**, or light **[Canvas](/en/materials/canvas)**. Modern versions use nylon.
- **Lines:** **[Silk](/en/materials/silk)** cord, thin **[Rope](/en/materials/rope)**, or high-strength twine.
- **Harness:** Heavy **[Canvas](/en/materials/canvas)** or **[Leather](/en/materials/leather)** straps.
- **Frame (Optional):** Some early designs used a **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** frame to keep the parachute open (more like a fixed umbrella).

## Variants and improvements

- **Round Parachute:** The simplest design; provides good drag but limited steerability.
- **Ram-Air Parachute:** A rectangular "wing" made of fabric cells that inflate with air. It acts like a glider, allowing the user to steer and flare for a soft landing.
- **Drogue Parachute:** A small parachute used to deploy a larger one or to provide stability at high speeds.
- **Pilot Chute:** A very small parachute that pulls the main canopy out of its container.

## Limits and risks

- **Opening Shock:** The sudden deceleration when the parachute opens can be violent and damage the equipment or injure the person.
- **Tangling:** If the lines cross or the canopy doesn't unfurl correctly ("candle"), the parachute will fail to provide drag.
- **Wind Drift:** Being lightweight and having a large surface area, parachutes are easily blown off course by wind.
- **Altitude:** Requires a minimum height to deploy and stabilize before reaching the ground.
