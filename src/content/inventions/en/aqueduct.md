# Brief description

An artificial channel built to transport water from a distant source to a point of use, such as a city, farm, or industrial site.

## Use / Function

- **Primary Use**: Supplying fresh water for drinking, irrigation, and baths.
- **Secondary Uses**: Powering water mills, mining, and waste removal (**[Sewage system](/en/inventions/sewage-system)**).
- **Scale**: Massive civil engineering projects spanning kilometers.

## Operating principle

- **Gravity Flow**: The entire system relies on a continuous and very gradual downward slope to keep the water moving.
- **Bridges and Siphons**: Arched bridges carry the channel across valleys, while inverted siphons (pressurized **[Pipes](/en/inventions/pipes)**) can cross deeper depressions.

## How to create it

1.  **Surveying**: Use tools like a **[Level](/en/inventions/level)** (chorobates) to find a source higher than the destination and plot a route with a constant gradient.
2.  **Tunnels**: Dig tunnels through hills to maintain a direct path and protect the water.
3.  **Construction**: Build the channel (specus) out of **[Stone](/en/materials/stone)** or **[Concrete](/en/materials/concrete)** lined with waterproof **[Mortar](/en/materials/mortar)**.
4.  **Bridges**: Construct arched bridges to cross valleys or rivers using the **[Arch](/en/inventions/arch)** principle.
5.  **Distribution**: At the destination, use a castellum (water tower) to settle debris and distribute water through **[Pipes](/en/inventions/pipes)**.

## Materials needed

- **Essential**: **[Stone](/en/materials/stone)**, **[Brick](/en/materials/brick)**, **[Concrete](/en/materials/concrete)**.
- **Waterproofing**: Hydraulic **[Mortar](/en/materials/mortar)**, **[Lead](/en/materials/lead)** (for pipes).
- **Tools**: **[Pickaxe](/en/inventions/pickaxe)**, **[Shovel](/en/inventions/shovel)**, **[Level](/en/inventions/level)**.

## Variants and improvements

- **Open Channel**: Simple ditch for irrigation (high evaporation).
- **Covered Channel**: Stone or concrete tunnel to prevent evaporation and contamination.
- **Pressurized Pipe**: Modern aqueducts often use pumps and pressure, allowing them to go uphill.

## Limits and risks

- **Gradient**: Too steep, and the water damages the structure; too shallow, and it stagnates.
- **Maintenance**: Mineral buildup (sinter) must be cleaned regularly.
- **Cost**: Extremely expensive and labor-intensive to build.
- **Vulnerability**: Strategic target in war (cutting off water supply).
