# Brief description

The abacus is a manual calculating tool that uses beads or stones moved along rods or grooves to perform arithmetic operations. It is one of the oldest known computational devices, predating the written Hindu-Arabic numeral system.

## Use / Function

- **Calculation**: Performing addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
- **Education**: Teaching the concepts of place value and arithmetic to students.
- **Commerce**: Rapidly calculating prices and totals in markets and shops.
- **Scale**: Individual and domestic use.

## Operating principle

The abacus exploits the concept of **place value**. Each rod or wire represents a different power of ten (units, tens, hundreds, thousands, etc.). By moving beads to represent numbers and following specific algorithms for shifting them during operations, complex calculations can be performed quickly.

## How to create it

- **Minimum functional version**: A series of grooves in the sand with small stones (calculi) moved within them.
- **Essential**: A frame to hold the components together and a set of movable counters.
- **Technical level**: Basic.

## Materials needed

- **Essential materials**:
  - **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)**: For the frame and possibly the rods.
  - **[Stone](/en/materials/stone)** or **[Clay](/en/materials/clay)**: For the beads or counters.
- **Tools**:
  - **[Knife](/en/inventions/knife)** or **[Saw](/en/inventions/saw)**: To shape the wood.
  - **[Drill](/en/inventions/hand-drill)**: To make holes in the beads.
- **Possible substitutes**:
  - **[Wire](/en/materials/wire)**: Instead of wooden rods for smoother movement.
  - **[String](/en/materials/string)**: To hold the beads if a rigid rod is not available.

## Variants and improvements

- **Roman Abacus**: Used grooves in a metal plate with sliding counters.
- **Suanpan (Chinese)**: Usually has two beads on the upper deck and five on the lower deck (2:5 system), allowing for hexadecimal calculations.
- **Soroban (Japanese)**: Improved version with a 1:4 bead configuration, optimized for the decimal system.
- **Schoty (Russian)**: Uses horizontal rods and a 10-bead system.

## Limits and risks

- **Training**: Requires significant practice to achieve high speeds and accuracy.
- **Manual Error**: A slip of the finger can change the result of a long calculation.
- **No Memory**: Unlike modern computers, it does not store intermediate results or steps unless recorded elsewhere.
