# Brief description

A crank is an arm attached at a right angle to a rotating shaft. It is one of the most important mechanical devices for converting reciprocating (back-and-forth) motion into circular motion, or vice versa.

## Use / Function

- **Motion Conversion:** Converts manual or piston-driven linear motion into rotation.
- **Mechanical Advantage:** Acts as a lever, allowing a smaller force to move a heavier load through a circular path.
- **Domestic Applications:** Used in hand-cranked mills, winches, and well windlasses.
- **Industrial Applications:** Essential for steam engines, internal combustion engines, and foot-powered lathes.

## Operating principle

The crank works on the principle of the lever. By applying force to the handle (the end of the arm), you create torque on the shaft. Because the handle travels in a larger circle than the shaft's diameter, you gain mechanical advantage. When coupled with a connecting rod, it allows a piston moving in a straight line to turn a wheel.

## How to create it

1.  **Shaft Preparation:** Start with a sturdy [Axle](/en/inventions/axle) or shaft.
2.  **Arm Attachment:** Attach a rigid arm at a 90-degree angle to one end of the shaft. This must be a very strong connection (using [Mechanical Joints](/en/inventions/mechanical-joints) or welding).
3.  **Handle/Offset:** Place a handle at the far end of the arm, parallel to the main shaft.
4.  **Balance:** In high-speed applications, a counterweight might be needed on the opposite side of the arm to prevent vibration.

## Materials needed

- **Shaft and Arm:** [Wood](/en/materials/wood) (for low stress), [Iron](/en/materials/iron) or [Steel](/en/materials/steel) (for high stress).
- **Bearings:** [Slide Bearings](/en/inventions/slide-bearings) to allow the shaft to rotate freely.
- **Fasteners:** [Nails and Mechanical Joints](/en/inventions/mechanical-joints) to secure the arm to the shaft.

## Variants and improvements

- **Simple Crank:** A single arm for manual use.
- **Compound Crank:** Multiple cranks on a single shaft (crankshaft), used in multi-cylinder engines.
- **Bell Crank:** Used to change the direction of motion, often in linkages.

## Limits and risks

- **Dead Centers:** In a single crank system, there are two points (top and bottom) where linear force cannot move the crank. A [Flywheel](/en/inventions/flywheel) is often needed to carry the motion through these points.
- **Stress:** The joint between the arm and the shaft is a major point of failure and must be exceptionally strong.
