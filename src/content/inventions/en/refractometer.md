# Brief description

A refractometer is an optical instrument used to measure the index of refraction of a substance, typically a liquid. By measuring how much light bends (refracts) as it passes through the sample, it can determine the concentration of dissolved substances.

## Use / Function

- **Quality Control:** Measuring sugar content in grapes for winemaking or in fruits for harvest.
- **Salinity Measurement:** Determining the salt concentration in seawater or aquariums.
- **Purity Testing:** Verifying the concentration of chemicals, coolants, or medications.
- **Medical Diagnostics:** Measuring the specific gravity of urine or protein levels in blood serum.

## Operating principle

The device works based on the **critical angle principle**:

1.  Light is passed through a thin layer of the sample liquid which is in contact with a high-refractive-index prism.
2.  Depending on the concentration of the liquid, the light will be refracted at a specific angle.
3.  The instrument projects a shadow line (the boundary between light and dark) onto a scale.
4.  The observer looks through an eyepiece to read the value where the shadow line intersects the scale.

## How to create it

1.  **Optical Prism:** Acquire or grind a high-quality **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** prism with a known refractive index.
2.  **Housing:** Construct a tube, typically made of **[Brass](/en/materials/brass)**, to hold the optics.
3.  **Sample Plate:** Attach a hinged transparent plate that allows a drop of liquid to be pressed into a thin film against the prism.
4.  **Scale:** Etch a precise scale onto a glass plate and place it at the focal point of the eyepiece.
5.  **Eyepiece:** Install a series of lenses to magnify the scale and the shadow line for the observer.
6.  **Calibration:** Adjust the device using distilled water (which should read zero on most scales).

## Materials needed

- **Prism:** High-refractive-index **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** (Flint glass).
- **Body:** **[Brass](/en/materials/brass)** or high-grade plastic tubing.
- **Lenses:** Small convex lenses for the eyepiece.
- **Reflector:** A small **[Mirror](/en/inventions/mirror)** to direct light into the prism.

## Variants and improvements

- **Abbe Refractometer:** A highly accurate laboratory benchtop instrument that uses two prisms.
- **Handheld Analog Refractometer:** A portable device used in the field (e.g., vineyards).
- **Digital Refractometer:** Uses an LED light source and an optical sensor to provide a digital readout, eliminating human error in reading the scale.

## Limits and risks

- **Temperature Sensitivity:** The refractive index changes with temperature; many modern devices include Automatic Temperature Compensation (ATC).
- **Opacity:** Samples must be transparent or translucent enough for light to pass through.
- **Corrosion:** Acidic or caustic samples can damage the prism surface over time if not cleaned immediately.
