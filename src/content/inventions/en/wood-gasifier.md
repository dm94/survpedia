# Brief description

A wood gasifier is a device that converts wood or other biomass into wood gas (syngas) through thermochemical gasification. This gas can be used as a fuel for heating, cooking, or powering internal combustion engines.

## Use / Function

- **Primary use:** Producing fuel for internal combustion engines in the absence of petroleum.
- **Secondary uses:** Heating, cooking, industrial heat processes.
- **Scale:** Portable (vehicle-mounted) to Small Industrial.

## Operating principle

**Gasification**: A process that converts organic materials into gas by reacting the material at high temperatures (>700°C), without combustion, with a controlled amount of oxygen and/or steam.
1.  **Drying:** Moisture is driven out of the wood.
2.  **Pyrolysis:** Wood breaks down into charcoal and volatile gases (tar).
3.  **Combustion:** Some of the charcoal burns to provide heat for the other stages.
4.  **Reduction:** CO2 and water vapor react with hot charcoal to form Carbon Monoxide (CO) and Hydrogen (H2).

## How to create it

1.  **Reaction Chamber:** A heavy-duty steel container (hearth) where the wood is heated.
2.  **Air Intake:** Controlled nozzles (tuyeres) to inject small amounts of air into the combustion zone.
3.  **Grate:** A support at the bottom to hold charcoal while allowing ash to fall through.
4.  **Cooling & Filtering:** The hot gas must be cooled and filtered to remove tars, soot, and moisture before entering an engine.
5.  **Inducer Fan:** A fan or engine vacuum to pull air through the system.

## Materials needed

- **Essential:** **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)** (must withstand high heat), **[Pipes](/en/inventions/pipes)**, insulation material (refractory brick or ash).
- **Tools:** **[Arc Welder](/en/inventions/arc-welder)** or mechanical fasteners, metal cutting tools.
- **Substitutes:** Repurposed steel drums (short life) or ceramic-lined containers.

## Variants and improvements

- **Updraft Gasifier:** Simple, but produces high tar; good for heating.
- **Downdraft Gasifier:** Forces gas through hot charcoal, breaking down tars; essential for engine use.
- **Crossdraft Gasifier:** Fast response, good for high-grade charcoal.

## Limits and risks

- **Tar Formation:** If not designed correctly, tars will condense and ruin an internal combustion engine.
- **Carbon Monoxide:** Wood gas is rich in CO, which is odorless, colorless, and deadly. Must be used in well-ventilated areas.
- **Fire Hazard:** Operates at very high temperatures; requires thermal shielding.
- **Maintenance:** Filters must be cleaned regularly; ash must be removed.
