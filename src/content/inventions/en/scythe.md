# Brief description

A scythe is an agricultural hand tool for mowing grass or reaping crops. It consists of a long curved blade mounted on a long wooden handle called a "snath." It is much more efficient than a sickle for large areas.

## Use / Function

- **Harvesting:** Cutting cereal crops (wheat, barley, oats) at the base.
- **Mowing:** Cutting grass for hay to feed [Livestock](/en/knowledge/livestock) during winter.
- **Vegetation Control:** Clearing large areas of weeds or brush.

## Operating principle

The scythe uses a long, sharp blade and the centrifugal force of a wide, swinging motion. The user stands with feet apart and swings the scythe in an arc from right to left. The curved blade slices through the stalks rather than hacking them. The geometry of the handle (snath) allows the user to work while standing upright, covering a large area with each stroke.

## How to create it

1.  **Blade Forging:** Forge a long, thin, curved blade from [Steel](/en/materials/steel). The edge must be very thin.
2.  **The Snath:** Carve a long handle (usually 1.5 to 2 meters) from a strong, lightweight [Wood](/en/materials/wood) like ash or willow. Often the snath is steam-bent to an S-shape for better ergonomics.
3.  **Grips:** Attach two short handles (nibs) to the snath, positioned for the user's hands to provide balance and control.
4.  **Assembly:** Secure the blade to the end of the snath using an iron ring and wedge or a bolt (a "scythe ring"). The angle of the blade (the "hang") is critical for efficiency.

## Materials needed

- **Blade:** High-carbon [Steel](/en/materials/steel) (for the edge) often laminated to [Iron](/en/materials/iron) for toughness.
- **Handle (Snath):** Ash, willow, or other tough [Wood](/en/materials/wood).
- **Fasteners:** [Iron](/en/materials/iron) rings or bolts.
- **Maintenance Tools:** A whetstone for sharpening in the field and a hammer/anvil for "peening" (cold-hammering) the edge.

## Variants and improvements

- **Sickle:** A short-handled, one-handed ancestor of the scythe.
- **Cradle Scythe:** A scythe with wooden "fingers" attached to the blade to collect the grain stalks neatly as they are cut, making binding into sheaves easier.

## Limits and risks

- **Skill Requirement:** Using a scythe effectively requires significant practice and rhythm.
- **Sharpening:** The blade must be sharpened frequently (every 15-30 minutes of use).
- **Injury:** The blade is extremely sharp and can cause severe injuries if the swing is uncontrolled or if the blade hits a hidden obstacle.
