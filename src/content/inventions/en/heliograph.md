# Brief description

A heliograph is a wireless telegraph that signals by flashes of sunlight reflected by a mirror. The flashes are produced by momentarily pivoting the mirror, or by interrupting the beam with a shutter. It was a primary means of long-range communication before the invention of radio.

## Use / Function

- **Long-distance communication:** Used to send messages over long distances (up to 50km or more) using Morse code or other flash codes.
- **Military use:** Widely used by armies for field communications in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
- **Surveying:** Used by surveyors for long-distance triangulation and establishing survey points.
- **Scale:** Regional / Military.

## Operating principle

- **Reflection:** It uses a plane **[Mirror](/en/inventions/mirror)** to reflect sunlight towards a distant station.
- **Collimation:** A sighting mechanism ensures the reflected beam is precisely aimed at the target station.
- **Modulation:** The continuous beam of light is broken into short and long flashes (representing dots and dashes) by either tilting the mirror or using a shutter.
- **Sun Alignment:** Because the sun moves, the mirror must be constantly adjusted to maintain the reflection on the target.

## How to create it

1.  **Mirror Assembly:** Mount a high-quality plane **[Mirror](/en/inventions/mirror)** (made of **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)**) on a sturdy tripod.
2.  **Sighting Mechanism:** Attach a sighting rod or a small telescope to align the mirror precisely with the receiving station.
3.  **Adjustment Screws:** Use **[Brass](/en/materials/brass)** or **[Iron](/en/materials/iron)** screws for fine adjustment of the mirror's altitude and azimuth.
4.  **Shutter or Key:** Install a spring-loaded key that tilts the mirror slightly when pressed, or a shutter that blocks the beam.
5.  **Secondary Mirror:** If the sun is behind the operator (relative to the target), use a second mirror to reflect the sunlight onto the primary signaling mirror.

## Materials needed

- **Reflector:** High-quality **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** mirror.
- **Frame/Tripod:** **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** or **[Iron](/en/materials/iron)** for stability.
- **Precision Parts:** **[Brass](/en/materials/brass)** for non-corrosive, precise moving parts and screws.
- **Tools:** **[Screw](/en/inventions/screw)**, Wrench, and alignment tools.

## Variants and improvements

- **Mance Heliograph:** A standard compact, portable version with a tilting mirror.
- **Begbie Heliostat:** A more complex version used for continuous signaling.
- **Heliostat:** A device that automatically tracks the sun to keep the beam reflected on a fixed target.
- **Aldis Lamp:** A handheld electric signaling lamp used for similar purposes at shorter ranges or at night.

## Limits and risks

- **Weather Dependent:** Completely useless at night, in cloudy weather, or in thick fog/smoke.
- **Line of Sight:** Requires a direct, unobstructed visual path between the two stations.
- **Security:** The flashes can be seen by anyone in the vicinity, potentially revealing the sender's location to enemies.
- **Earth's Curvature:** Limits the maximum range based on the height of the stations.
