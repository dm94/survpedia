# Brief description

A bicycle is a human-powered, pedal-driven, single-track vehicle, having two wheels attached to a frame, one behind the other. It is one of the most efficient means of transportation ever devised, converting human energy into motion with minimal loss.

## Use / Function

Its primary purpose is efficient personal transportation:

- **Transportation:** Moving people and small loads over moderate distances.
- **Efficiency:** Much faster than walking with the same energy expenditure.
- **Low Maintenance:** Requires no fuel and simple mechanical care.
- **Accessibility:** Can be used on narrow paths where larger vehicles cannot pass.

## Operating principle

The bicycle operates on several mechanical and physical principles:

1. **Balance and Steering:** Stability is maintained through a combination of gyroscopic effects from the spinning wheels and the rider's active balancing (countersteering).
2. **Drive Train:** Pedals turn a crank, which drives a **[chain](/en/inventions/chain)** connected to the rear wheel. This converts the reciprocating motion of the legs into rotational motion.
3. **Mechanical Advantage:** Using **[gears](/en/inventions/gears)** or different sprocket sizes allows the rider to trade speed for torque, making it easier to climb hills.
4. **Pneumatic Tires:** Rubber tires filled with air (natural rubber) provide cushioning and traction.

## How to create it

- **Step 1: Frame Construction:** Build a diamond-shaped frame using **[steel](/en/materials/steel)** tubes for strength and lightness.
- **Step 2: Wheels:** Construct wheels with a central hub, spokes (under tension), and a rim.
- **Step 3: Drive System:** Install a crankset, pedals, and a **[chain](/en/inventions/chain)** to transfer power to the rear hub.
- **Step 4: Steering:** Attach a front fork and handlebars to the frame using bearings.
- **Step 5: Braking:** Install a friction-based braking system (pads against the rim).

## Materials needed

- **Frame and Components:** **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)** or high-strength alloys.
- **Tires:** **[Natural rubber](/en/materials/natural-rubber)** and fabric (cotton or nylon).
- **Saddle:** **[Leather](/en/materials/leather)** or padded plastic.
- **Bearings:** Hardened steel balls for hubs, bottom bracket, and headset.
- **Drive:** Steel **[chain](/en/inventions/chain)** and **[gears](/en/inventions/gears)**.

## Variants and improvements

- **Draisine (Laufmaschine):** The earliest version (1817), with no pedals; the rider pushed off the ground.
- **Penny-farthing:** Had a giant front wheel for speed before chain drives were perfected.
- **Safety Bicycle:** The modern design with two equal-sized wheels and a chain drive.
- **Mountain Bikes:** Built with reinforced frames and wide tires for off-road use.
- **Electric Bicycles:** Adds a motor and **[battery](/en/inventions/battery)** to assist the rider.

## Limits and risks

- **Rider Skill:** Requires balance and coordination to operate safely.
- **Exposure:** Provides no protection from weather or collisions.
- **Terrain:** Efficiency drops significantly on very soft ground (sand, deep mud) or extremely steep inclines.
- **Mechanical Failure:** Chain breaks or tire punctures can strand the rider.
