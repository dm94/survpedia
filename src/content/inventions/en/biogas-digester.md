# Brief description

A biogas digester is an airtight container that facilitates the anaerobic digestion of organic matter (such as manure, food scraps, or plant material) by bacteria, producing biogas (a mixture of methane and carbon dioxide) and a nutrient-rich slurry (digestate).

## Use / Function

- **Primary use:** Producing renewable fuel for cooking, heating, or lighting.
- **Secondary uses:** Producing high-quality organic fertilizer (slurry); waste management and sanitation.
- **Scale:** Domestic (household level) to Industrial (large-scale farms).

## Operating principle

**Anaerobic digestion**: In the absence of oxygen, specialized bacteria break down complex organic compounds.
1.  **Hydrolysis:** Large molecules are broken down into simpler ones.
2.  **Acidogenesis/Acetogenesis:** Conversion into organic acids and alcohols.
3.  **Methanogenesis:** Archaea convert these into methane (CH4) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

## How to create it

1.  **Construction:** Build an airtight tank (digester). It can be a fixed-dome (concrete/brick) or floating-drum (metal/plastic) design.
2.  **Feeding System:** Install an inlet pipe to feed the organic waste slurry (mixed with water).
3.  **Gas Collection:** Install an outlet at the top of the tank to capture the gas.
4.  **Effluent Outlet:** Install an overflow pipe to remove the processed slurry.
5.  **Sealing:** Ensure absolute airtightness; any leak will prevent gas accumulation and allow oxygen in, stopping the process.

## Materials needed

- **Essential:** Airtight tank (**[Concrete](/en/materials/concrete)**, **[Brick](/en/materials/brick)**, **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)**, or **[Plastic](/en/materials/plastic)**), **[Pipes](/en/inventions/pipes)** (PVC or Metal), valves.
- **Tools:** Masonry tools, plumbing tools.
- **Substitutes:** Repurposed oil drums or large water tanks for small-scale digesters.

## Variants and improvements

- **Fixed-dome:** Underground, durable, no moving parts, but requires masonry skill.
- **Floating-drum:** Easier to monitor gas volume, maintains constant pressure, but the metal drum can corrode.
- **Bag digester:** Flexible PVC or PE bag, cheap and easy to install but prone to punctures.

## Limits and risks

- **Temperature:** Bacteria are sensitive; the process slows down below 20°C and stops near freezing.
- **pH Balance:** Overfeeding can make the slurry too acidic, "souring" the digester.
- **Explosion Risk:** Biogas is flammable; leaks in enclosed spaces are dangerous.
- **H2S Content:** Biogas contains small amounts of hydrogen sulfide, which is corrosive and toxic; it may require scrubbing (e.g., through iron filings).
