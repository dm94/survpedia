# Brief description

A biogas digester (or biodigester) is an airtight container where organic waste is decomposed by bacteria in the absence of oxygen to produce **[Biogas](/en/materials/biogas)**. It is a sustainable technology that converts waste into clean energy and nutrient-rich fertilizer (biol).

## Use / Function

- **Waste Management:** Safe disposal of animal manure, human waste, and organic kitchen scraps.
- **Energy Production:** Generating **[Biogas](/en/materials/biogas)** for cooking, heating, or lighting.
- **Fertilizer Production:** Producing liquid and solid organic fertilizer (digestate/biol) as a byproduct.
- **Sanitation:** Reducing pathogens and odors associated with raw organic waste.
- **Scale:** Household (small) to Industrial (large).

## Operating principle

1.  **Feeding:** Organic waste is mixed with **[Water](/en/materials/water)** to form a slurry and fed into the digester.
2.  **Anaerobic Digestion:** Inside the airtight chamber, anaerobic bacteria break down the organic matter through four stages: hydrolysis, acidogenesis, acetogenesis, and methanogenesis.
3.  **Gas Collection:** The produced **[Biogas](/en/materials/biogas)** rises to the top of the chamber and is collected through **[Pipes](/en/inventions/pipes)** or a **[Hose](/en/inventions/hose)**.
4.  **Effluent Discharge:** As new slurry is added, an equal volume of fermented liquid (biol) is pushed out through an outlet, ready to be used as fertilizer.

## How to create it

### 1. Fixed-Dome Digester (Durable)
- **Structure:** An underground airtight dome made of **[Brick](/en/materials/brick)** and **[Cement](/en/materials/cement)**.
- **Mechanism:** The gas pressure pushes the slurry into a separate compensation tank. When gas is used, the slurry flows back.
- **Level:** Intermediate/Advanced (requires masonry skills).

### 2. Plastic Bag Digester (Simple/Low Cost)
- **Structure:** A long, tubular plastic or rubber bag.
- **Mechanism:** The bag expands as gas is produced.
- **Level:** Basic.

### 3. IBC Tote Digester (Portable/Modular)
- **Structure:** Using large industrial plastic containers (IBC totes).
- **Level:** Basic/Intermediate.

## Materials needed

- **Chamber:** **[Brick](/en/materials/brick)**, **[Stone](/en/materials/stone)**, **[Cement](/en/materials/cement)**, or high-density plastic/rubber.
- **Sealant:** **[Clay](/en/materials/clay)**, bitumen, or specialized waterproofing cement.
- **Plumbing:** **[Pipes](/en/inventions/pipes)** (PVC or metal) and **[Hoses](/en/inventions/hose)** for gas and slurry transport.
- **Valves:** To control gas flow and prevent backflow.
- **Organic Waste:** Manure, food scraps, or plant material.

## Variants and improvements

- **Continuous Flow:** New waste is added daily, and gas/fertilizer is produced continuously.
- **Batch Digester:** The digester is filled, sealed until gas production stops, then emptied and refilled.
- **Heating:** In cold climates, the digester may need insulation or a heating system to keep the bacteria active.
- **Agitation:** Stirring the slurry prevents the formation of a "scum" layer on top, improving gas yield.

## Limits and risks

- **Temperature:** Production slows down or stops below 15°C; requires warm conditions.
- **Airtightness:** Any leak will prevent gas collection and can be dangerous.
- **Loading:** Overfeeding or adding toxic substances (like detergents or antibiotics) can kill the bacteria.
- **Explosion Risk:** If **[Biogas](/en/materials/biogas)** leaks in a confined space, it can be explosive.
- **Maintenance:** Requires regular removal of accumulated sludge and checking for leaks.
