# Brief description

A biogas digester is a system that decomposes organic waste (like manure or food scraps) in the absence of oxygen to produce **[Biogas](/en/materials/biogas)** and a nutrient-rich fertilizer.

## Use / Function

- **Primary use**: Production of fuel for cooking, lighting, or heating.
- **Secondary uses**: Waste management, production of high-quality liquid fertilizer (slurry).
- **Scale**: Small household units to large industrial facilities.

## Operating principle

**Anaerobic Digestion**:

1.  **Loading**: Organic waste and water are mixed (forming a slurry) and fed into an airtight tank.
2.  **Digestion**: Bacteria (methanogens) break down the organic matter in an environment without oxygen.
3.  **Gas Production**: The process releases a mixture of methane and carbon dioxide (**[Biogas](/en/materials/biogas)**).
4.  **Gas Collection**: The gas rises and is collected in a storage tank or a floating dome.
5.  **Effluent**: The processed slurry (digestate) is pushed out of the outlet and can be used as fertilizer.

## How to create it

### 1. The Tank

- Construct an airtight container using **[Concrete](/en/materials/concrete)**, **[Plastic](/en/materials/plastic)**, or **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)**.
- It must have an inlet pipe for waste, an outlet pipe for digestate, and a gas outlet at the top.

### 2. The Gas Holder

- A common design is the "floating drum," where an inverted drum sits in the slurry and rises as gas accumulates.
- Alternatively, a fixed-dome design uses the pressure of the gas to displace the slurry.

### 3. Operation

- Maintain a warm temperature (ideally 30-40°C) for the bacteria.
- Keep the pH balanced (not too acidic).

## Materials needed

- **Tank Material**: **[Concrete](/en/materials/concrete)**, **[Brick](/en/materials/brick)**, or high-density **[Plastic](/en/materials/plastic)**.
- **Sealing**: **[Bitumen](/en/materials/bitumen)** or specialized sealants to ensure airtightness.
- **Pipes**: **[PVC](/en/materials/plastic)** or metal **[Pipes](/en/inventions/pipes)** for gas and waste transport.
- **Input**: Organic waste (animal manure, food waste, crop residues) and **[Water](/en/materials/water)**.

## Variants and improvements

- **Fixed Dome**: A permanent masonry structure with no moving parts.
- **Floating Drum**: Easier to monitor gas levels but more prone to corrosion.
- **Bag Digester**: A simple, flexible plastic bag used in warmer climates.

## Limits and risks

- **Explosion Risk**: Methane is flammable; the system must be airtight and kept away from open flames.
- **Temperature Sensitivity**: Gas production drops significantly in cold weather.
- **Pathogens**: While digestion reduces pathogens, the slurry should still be handled with care.
- **Hydrogen Sulfide**: Biogas contains small amounts of H2S, which is toxic and corrosive; it may need to be filtered through iron filings.
