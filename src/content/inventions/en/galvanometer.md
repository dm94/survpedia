# Brief description

A galvanometer is an instrument for detecting and measuring small electric currents. It is the fundamental diagnostic tool for understanding and utilizing **[Electricity](/en/knowledge/electricity)**.

## Use / Function

- **Current Detection:** Verifying if a circuit is active.
- **Polarity Testing:** Determining the direction of current flow.
- **Component Building:** Base for making voltmeters and ammeters.
- **Research:** Essential for experiments with electromagnetism.

## Operating principle

The galvanometer works on the principle of **electromagnetism**:
1. An electric current flowing through a coil of wire produces a magnetic field.
2. This magnetic field exerts a force on a nearby magnetic needle.
3. The deflection of the needle is proportional to the strength and direction of the current.

## How to create it

### Minimum functional version
1.  **Base:** A non-conductive base made of **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** or plastic.
2.  **Magnetic Needle:** A magnetized **[Iron](/en/materials/iron)** needle (compass needle) balanced on a pivot.
3.  **Coil:** Wrap many turns of insulated **[Copper](/en/materials/copper)** wire around the needle or a frame surrounding the needle.
4.  **Alignment:** Align the coil so that when no current flows, the needle points parallel to the loops of the coil.

## Materials needed

- **Essential materials:**
  - Insulated **[Copper](/en/materials/copper)** wire (the thinner and longer, the more sensitive).
  - Magnetized needle (made from **[Iron](/en/materials/iron)** or using **[Magnetite](/en/materials/magnetite)**).
  - **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** or cardboard for the frame and base.
- **Tools:**
  - **[Knife](/en/inventions/knife)** for stripping insulation.
  - **[Compass](/en/inventions/compass)** (can serve as the needle assembly).

## Variants and improvements

- **Astatic Galvanometer:** Uses two needles with opposite polarities to cancel out the Earth's magnetic field, greatly increasing sensitivity.
- **Moving Coil (D'Arsonval):** The coil moves instead of the magnet; more robust and accurate for modern use.
- **Mirror Galvanometer:** Uses a tiny mirror and a beam of light to amplify small deflections.

## Limits and risks

- **Magnetic Interference:** Nearby magnets or iron objects can distort readings.
- **Overload:** Excessively high current can melt the fine wire of the coil or demagnetize the needle.
- **Earth's Magnetic Field:** Simple versions must be oriented correctly relative to North to function as intended.
