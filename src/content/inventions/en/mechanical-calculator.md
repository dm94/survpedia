# Brief description

A mechanical device used to perform the basic operations of arithmetic automatically. It replaces mental calculation or manual tools like the abacus with a system of interlocking gears and levers that track numerical values.

## Use / Function

- **Primary use:** Performing addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division with high accuracy.
- **Secondary uses:** Accounting, navigation (logarithmic calculations), and scientific data processing.
- **Scale:** Desktop devices for businesses, engineers, and scientists.

## Operating principle

Mechanical calculators rely on the "stepped drum" or "pinwheel" mechanisms to represent and manipulate numbers:

1.  **Input:** Numbers are set using sliders, keys, or wheels.
2.  **Addition:** Turning a crank rotates gears. A "carry" mechanism ensures that when a gear completes a full rotation (representing 0-9), it advances the next gear (the tens place) by one tooth.
3.  **Subtraction:** Performed by reversing the rotation or using complementary addition.
4.  **Multiplication/Division:** Accomplished through repeated addition or subtraction combined with shifting the carriage (changing the decimal place).

## How to create it

A mechanical calculator is one of the most complex mechanical devices to build, requiring extreme precision.

1.  **Digit Wheels:** A series of cylinders or disks marked 0-9.
2.  **Carry Mechanism:** The most difficult part; a "tens-carry" lever or cam that triggers the next digit wheel at exactly the right moment without jamming the system.
3.  **Accumulator:** A register that displays the result of the calculations.
4.  **Reset Mechanism:** A way to quickly return all digit wheels to zero.
5.  **Casing:** A sturdy box, often **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** or metal, to protect the delicate internal works.

## Materials needed

- **Gears and Mechanisms:** **[Brass](/en/materials/brass)** is preferred for its ease of machining and low friction. **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)** is used for high-wear parts like springs and pawls.
- **Axles:** Polished **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)** rods.
- **Frame:** **[Iron](/en/materials/iron)** or **[Brass](/en/materials/brass)** plates to hold the axles in perfect alignment.
- **Lubrication:** Fine **[Vegetable Oil](/en/materials/vegetable-oil)** or animal fats to ensure smooth operation.

## Variants and improvements

- **Pascaline:** One of the earliest calculators (17th century), primarily for addition and subtraction.
- **Arithmometer:** The first commercially successful mechanical calculator, capable of all four arithmetic operations.
- **Comptometer:** A key-driven calculator where the act of pressing the keys provides the power for the calculation.
- **Difference Engine:** A massive, specialized mechanical calculator designed to tabulate polynomial functions (early mechanical computer).

## Limits and risks

- **Precision:** A tiny error in the shape of a gear tooth can cause the entire machine to jam or provide incorrect results.
- **Complexity:** Extremely difficult to repair if an internal part breaks.
- **Speed:** Much slower than electronic calculators; complex multiplications can take significant physical effort.
- **Size:** Capable machines are often heavy and bulky.
