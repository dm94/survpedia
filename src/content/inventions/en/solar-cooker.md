# Brief description

A solar cooker is a device which uses the energy of direct sunlight to heat, cook or pasteurize drink and other food materials. It requires no fuel and costs nothing to operate.

## Use / Function

- **Primary use**: Cooking food and boiling water without fuel.
- **Secondary uses**: Pasteurizing water to make it safe to drink, sterilizing medical instruments.
- **Scale**: Portable units for families to large-scale communal cookers.

## Operating principle

**Concentration, Absorption, and Retention**:

1.  **Concentration**: Reflective surfaces (mirrors or polished metal) redirect sunlight toward a central cooking area.
2.  **Absorption**: A black or dark-colored container absorbs the concentrated solar energy and converts it into heat.
3.  **Retention**: A transparent cover (glass or plastic) allows sunlight to enter but traps the heat inside (the greenhouse effect), while insulation prevents heat from escaping.

## How to create it

### 1. The Box Cooker (Simplest)

- Build two nested wooden or cardboard boxes with insulation (like straw or wool) between them.
- Line the inner box with black paint or blackened metal.
- Place a glass lid on top.
- Attach a reflective lid (mirror or aluminum foil) to redirect more light into the box.

### 2. The Parabolic Cooker

- Shape a reflective surface (like a satellite dish lined with foil) into a parabola.
- Mount a pot at the focal point where the rays converge. This achieves much higher temperatures but requires frequent tracking of the sun.

## Materials needed

- **Reflective Material**: **[Mirror](/en/materials/mirror)**, polished **[Aluminum](/en/materials/aluminum)**, or tin foil.
- **Absorptive Surface**: Matte black paint or charcoal-blackened metal.
- **Glazing**: **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** or clear plastic sheets.
- **Insulation**: **[Wool](/en/materials/wool)**, straw, or **[Mineral Wool](/en/materials/mineral-wool)**.
- **Structure**: **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)**, cardboard, or **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)**.

## Variants and improvements

- **Solar Still**: A variant used specifically for distilling water. See **[Solar Still](/en/inventions/solar-still)**.
- **Community Cooker**: Large-scale versions that can feed hundreds of people.
- **Heat Storage**: Using stones or salt to store heat for cooking after sunset.

## Limits and risks

- **Weather Dependent**: Only works in direct, bright sunlight.
- **Cooking Time**: Generally slower than fire-based cooking.
- **Eye Safety**: Concentrated sunlight can cause permanent eye damage; always use sunglasses or avoid looking at the focal point.
- **Burns**: The cooking vessel can reach very high temperatures.
