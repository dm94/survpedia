# Brief description

A solar cooker is a device that uses the energy of direct sunlight to heat, cook, or pasteurize drinkable liquids and other food materials. It requires no fuel and costs nothing to operate, making it an essential technology for resource-scarce environments.

## Use / Function

- **Cooking:** Boiling, baking, and roasting food.
- **Water Pasteurization:** Heating water to at least 65°C (149°F) to kill disease-causing organisms.
- **Sterilization:** Disinfecting medical instruments or bandages in emergencies.
- **Scale:** Domestic (individual or family use).

## Operating principle

Solar cookers work by concentrating, absorbing, and retaining solar energy:
1.  **Concentration:** Reflective surfaces (mirrors or polished metal) redirect sunlight onto a central cooking area.
2.  **Absorption:** A black or dark-colored cooking pot absorbs the concentrated light and converts it into heat.
3.  **Retention:** A transparent cover (glass or plastic) allows sunlight in but traps the heat inside (the greenhouse effect), while insulation prevents heat from escaping.

## How to create it

### Box Cooker (Basic/Intermediate)

1.  **Outer Box:** A large box made of **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** or thick cardboard.
2.  **Inner Box:** A smaller box that fits inside the outer box, leaving a gap for insulation.
3.  **Insulation:** Fill the gap between the boxes with crumpled paper, straw, or wool.
4.  **Absorber:** Paint the inside of the inner box with non-toxic matte black **[Paint](/en/materials/paint)**.
5.  **Glazing:** Cover the top with a sheet of **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** or clear plastic.
6.  **Reflector:** Attach a lid or panel lined with **[Aluminum](/en/materials/aluminum)** foil to reflect additional sunlight into the box.

## Materials needed

- **Reflectors:** **[Aluminum](/en/materials/aluminum)** foil, mirrors, or polished **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)**.
- **Structure:** **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)**, **[Paper](/en/materials/paper)** (cardboard), or wicker.
- **Glazing:** **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** or heat-resistant plastic film.
- **Insulation:** Straw, wool, crumpled **[Paper](/en/materials/paper)**, or sawdust.
- **Cooking Pot:** Thin-walled metal pot painted black on the outside.

## Variants and improvements

- **Panel Cooker:** Uses multiple reflective panels to concentrate light on a pot enclosed in a heat-resistant bag. Simple and portable.
- **Parabolic Cooker:** Uses a curved reflective dish to focus light on a single point. Reaches very high temperatures (comparable to a conventional stove) but requires frequent adjustment and can be dangerous to eyes.
- **Vacuum Tube Cooker:** Uses a double-walled glass tube with a vacuum in between for near-perfect insulation. Highly efficient even in cold weather.

## Limits and risks

- **Weather Dependent:** Only works in direct sunlight; useless at night or on very cloudy days.
- **Cooking Time:** Generally slower than traditional fire-based cooking.
- **Eye Safety:** Concentrated sunlight from parabolic reflectors can cause permanent eye damage. Use sunglasses.
- **Orientation:** Must be periodically repositioned to follow the movement of the sun.
