# Brief description

A solar cooker is a device which uses the energy of direct sunlight to heat, cook or pasteurize drinks and other food materials. It allows cooking without fuel, which is vital in resource-scarce environments.

## Use / Function

- **Cooking:** Boiling, baking, and roasting food.
- **Water Pasteurization:** Making water safe to drink by heating it to 65°C (149°F).
- **Sterilization:** Disinfecting medical instruments in high-performance models.
- **Scale:** Domestic to community-sized.

## Operating principle

Solar cookers work by concentrating, absorbing, and retaining solar energy:

1.  **Concentration:** Reflective surfaces (mirrors or polished metal) redirect sunlight toward a central cooking area.
2.  **Absorption:** A dark-colored (preferably black) container absorbs the concentrated light and converts it into heat.
3.  **Retention:** A transparent cover (glass or plastic) allows light to enter but traps heat inside (the greenhouse effect), while insulation prevents heat loss to the environment.

## How to create it

1.  **Technical Level:** Basic.
2.  **Outer Box:** Construct a wooden or cardboard box.
3.  **Inner Box:** Place a slightly smaller box inside, painted black on the inside to absorb heat.
4.  **Insulation:** Fill the gap between the boxes with crumpled paper, wool, or straw to prevent heat loss.
5.  **Glazing:** Cover the top of the inner box with a sheet of **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)**.
6.  **Reflector:** Attach a lid with a **[Mirror](/en/inventions/mirror)** or polished **[Aluminum](/en/materials/aluminum)** foil to bounce extra light into the box.

## Materials needed

- **Reflector:** **[Mirror](/en/inventions/mirror)** or polished **[Aluminum](/en/materials/aluminum)**.
- **Glazing:** **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** or heat-resistant plastic.
- **Absorber:** **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)** pot painted with matte black **[Paint](/en/materials/paint)**.
- **Structure:** **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** or cardboard.
- **Insulation:** **[Wool](/en/materials/wool)**, straw, or sawdust.

## Variants and improvements

- **Parabolic Cooker:** Uses a curved reflector to focus light on a single point. Reaches much higher temperatures but requires frequent adjustment to track the sun.
- **Panel Cooker:** Uses multiple flat reflective panels to redirect light onto a pot inside a plastic bag (acting as glazing).
- **Solar Oven:** A highly insulated box cooker that can reach temperatures high enough for baking bread.

## Limits and risks

- **Weather Dependent:** Only works in direct, bright sunlight.
- **Cooking Time:** Generally takes longer than traditional fire or gas stoves.
- **Eye Safety:** Concentrated sunlight from parabolic reflectors can cause permanent eye damage; use sunglasses.
- **Burns:** Surfaces and pots can become extremely hot.
