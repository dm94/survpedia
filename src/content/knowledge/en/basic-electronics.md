# Brief description

Basic electronics is the branch of physics and technology concerned with the design of circuits using transistors and microchips, and the behavior and movement of electrons in a semiconductor, conductor, vacuum, or gas. Unlike basic electricity, which focuses on power transmission, electronics focuses on the control and manipulation of electric current to process information.

## Use / Function

- **Signal Processing:** Amplifying weak signals (radio, sound).
- **Communication:** Encoding and decoding information for transmission.
- **Computing:** Performing logical operations using electronic switches.
- **Control Systems:** Automatically regulating machines based on sensor input.

## Operating principle

Electronics relies on components that can control the flow of current in ways beyond a simple on-off switch.

- **Conduction and Insulation:** Using **[Copper](/en/materials/copper)** for paths and **[Rubber](/en/materials/natural-rubber)** or **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** to prevent leakage.
- **Semiconductors:** Materials like **[Silicon](/en/materials/silicon)** or **[Germanium](/en/materials/germanium)** that can be made to conduct electricity under certain conditions.
- **Active Components:** Devices like **[Transistors](/en/inventions/transistor)** or **[Vacuum Tubes](/en/inventions/vacuum-tube)** that can use a small signal to control a much larger current (amplification).
- **Passive Components:**
    - **Resistors:** Limit current flow and drop voltage.
    - **[Capacitors](/en/inventions/capacitor):** Store charge and block direct current while passing alternating current.
    - **Inductors:** Store energy in a magnetic field and oppose changes in current.

## How to implement it

1.  **Circuit Design:** Plan the path of current and the placement of components to achieve a goal (e.g., an amplifier).
2.  **Substrate:** Use an insulating board (traditionally **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)**, **[Paper](/en/materials/paper)**-based phenolics, or fiberglass) to mount components.
3.  **Connection:** Use wires or conductive traces to link components. Soldering is the standard method for permanent connection.
4.  **Power:** Provide a stable voltage source (Battery or regulated Power Supply).

## Materials needed

- **Conductors:** **[Copper](/en/materials/copper)** wire, solder (tin/lead alloy).
- **Insulators:** **[Ceramics](/en/materials/ceramics)**, **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)**, plastics.
- **Semiconductors:** **[Silicon](/en/materials/silicon)**, **[Germanium](/en/materials/germanium)**.
- **Tools:** Soldering iron, wire cutters, pliers, multi-meter (for measurement).

## Variants and improvements

- **Analog Electronics:** Deals with continuous signals (sound, temperature).
- **Digital Electronics:** Deals with discrete states (0 and 1), the basis of modern computers.
- **Integrated Circuits (ICs):** Shrinking thousands or millions of components onto a single tiny chip of silicon.

## Limits and risks

- **Heat:** Electronic components generate heat and can be destroyed if it is not dissipated (using heat sinks).
- **Electrostatic Discharge (ESD):** Static electricity can instantly destroy sensitive semiconductor components.
- **Complexity:** Small errors in a complex circuit can be extremely difficult to diagnose.
- **Sensitivity:** Electronic circuits can be disrupted by external electromagnetic interference (EMI).
