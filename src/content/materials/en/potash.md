Potash is a general term for various mined and manufactured salts that contain potassium in water-soluble form. Historically, it was produced by leaching the ashes of burnt wood and evaporating the resulting solution.

## Description of what it is like

Potash is typically found as a white or greyish-white crystalline powder or granular solid. It has a salty, alkaline taste and is highly soluble in water. It is caustic in concentrated forms.

## Origin and where to find it

- **Environments**: Can be manufactured wherever there is an abundant supply of wood or certain types of seaweed.
- **Signs**: Found in the residue of large fires, especially from hardwoods.
- **Natural/Synthetic**: Synthetic (manufactured from natural ash).

### Minimum processing required

- **Burning**: Burn wood or seaweed to produce ash.
- **Leaching**: Soak the ash in water to dissolve the potassium salts.
- **Filtration**: Strain the liquid (lye) to remove insoluble ash particles.
- **Evaporation**: Boil the liquid in a large pot until only the solid salts remain.
- **Calcination**: Heat the solid residue to burn off impurities, resulting in "pearl ash."

### Tools needed to work on it

- **Large iron pot**: For evaporation and calcination.
- **Water barrels**: For the leaching process.
- **Straining cloth**: To filter the lye.
- **Fuel**: Significant amounts of wood or coal for the boiling process.

### Common forms of use

- **Agricultural Fertilizer**: Provides essential potassium to plants.
- **Flux**: Lowers the melting point of silica in [Glass](/en/materials/glass) making.
- **Saponification**: Reacted with [Animal Fat](/en/materials/animal-fat) to create [Soap](/en/materials/soap).

### Possible substitutes

- **Soda Ash (Sodium Carbonate)**: Often interchangeable in glass and soap making.
- **Kelp Ash**: Specifically for iodine and potash.

### Limitations and common failures

- **Purity**: If not properly calcined, it remains dark and full of carbon impurities.
- **Storage**: Highly hygroscopic (absorbs moisture from the air), so it must be kept in sealed containers.

### Risks and safety

- **Caustic**: Concentrated lye and potash can cause chemical burns to skin and eyes.
- **Heavy fuel use**: Historically led to significant deforestation.
