Manure is organic matter, mostly derived from animal feces, used as organic fertilizer in agriculture.

## Description of what it is like

A mix of animal waste and often bedding material (like straw). It is moist when fresh and can be dried into cakes.

## Origin and where to find it

- Obtained from domesticated animals like cows, horses, pigs, and chickens.

### Minimum processing required

- Collection and storage.
- Composting is recommended to reduce pathogens and weed seeds.

### Tools needed to work on it

- **[Shovel](/en/inventions/shovel)** and pitchfork.
- **[Wheelbarrow](/en/inventions/wheelbarrow)** for transport.

### Common forms of use

- **Fertilizer**: Spread on fields to improve soil structure and nutrients.
- **Biogas Feedstock**: Mixed with water in a digester.
- **Fuel**: Dried dung cakes are used for cooking in many parts of the world.

### Possible substitutes

- **[Compost](/en/knowledge/compost)**: Decayed plant matter.
- **[Synthetic Fertilizer](/en/materials/synthetic-fertilizer)**: Industrially produced.

### Limitations and common failures

- Odor: Can be very strong.
- Pathogens: Can contain harmful bacteria if not handled correctly.

### Risks and safety

- Disease: Potential for zoonotic diseases.
- Ammonia: Can release irritating gases in confined spaces.
