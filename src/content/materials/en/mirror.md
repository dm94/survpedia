A mirror is an object that reflects an image.

## Description of what it is like

Usually a flat sheet of glass with a dark or metallic backing that creates a clear reflection.

## Origin and where to find it

- Must be manufactured. Requires high-quality **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)** and metals like **[Silver](/en/materials/silver)** or **[Aluminum](/en/materials/aluminum)**.

### Minimum processing required

- Polishing glass to a very flat surface.
- Applying a thin layer of metal to one side.
- Adding a protective backing paint.

### Tools needed to work on it

- Glass cutters.
- Chemical baths for silvering.

### Common forms of use

- **Plane Mirror**: Standard flat mirror.
- **Curved Mirror**: Concave or convex for focusing or spreading light.

### Possible substitutes

- Polished **[Metal](/en/materials/metal)** plates (less efficient).
- Still **[Water](/en/materials/water)** surface.

### Limitations and common failures

- Fragility: Easily broken.
- Tarnishing: The reflective layer can corrode over time if not sealed.

### Risks and safety

- Sharp edges: Broken glass is extremely dangerous.
