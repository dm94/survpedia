# Description of what it is like

Bakelite (polyoxybenzylmethylenglycolanhydride) was the first plastic made from synthetic components. It is a thermosetting phenol formaldehyde resin. Once set, it cannot be melted or reshaped, making it exceptionally durable and heat-resistant compared to later thermoplastics.

## Origin and where to find it

- **Synthetic:** Created by the reaction of phenol (derived from coal tar) and formaldehyde (derived from wood alcohol/methanol).
- **History:** Invented by Leo Baekeland in 1907.
- **Scavenging:** Found in vintage electrical equipment, old telephones, radios, and heavy-duty kitchenware handles from the early to mid-20th century.

### Minimum processing required

- **Synthesis:** Requires a controlled chemical reaction between phenol and formaldehyde in the presence of a catalyst (usually an acid or base).
- **Molding:** The resulting resin is ground into a powder, mixed with fillers (like wood flour or asbestos for strength), and then cured in a heated mold under high pressure.

### Tools needed to work on it

- **Chemical Reactor:** To safely handle the volatile and toxic precursors.
- **Heated Press:** A high-pressure mold capable of reaching temperatures around 150-200°C.
- **Finishing Tools:** Files, sandpaper, and polishing buffs for shaping and smoothing the cured parts.

### Common forms of use

- **Electrical Insulation:** Used for switches, sockets, and insulators due to its non-conductive and heat-resistant properties.
- **Consumer Goods:** Housings for early radios, cameras, and telephones.
- **Kitchenware:** Pot handles and knobs that stay cool to the touch.

### Possible substitutes

- **Modern Plastics:** Phenolic resins, epoxy, or high-temperature thermoplastics.
- **Natural Insulators:** **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)** (dry), **[Glass](/en/materials/glass)**, **[Ceramics](/en/materials/ceramics)**, or ebonite (hard rubber).

### Limitations and common failures

- **Brittleness:** It is a rigid material and can crack or shatter if dropped or subjected to heavy impact.
- **Odor:** When heated or rubbed vigorously, it may emit a distinctive "phenolic" (chemical/medicinal) smell.
- **Non-Recyclable:** As a thermoset, it cannot be melted down and reused like modern water bottles.

### Risks and safety

- **Toxic Precursors:** Phenol and formaldehyde are both toxic and carcinogenic. Synthesis must be performed with extreme care and proper ventilation.
- **Asbestos Fillers:** Some vintage Bakelite items contain asbestos fibers. Sanding or breaking these can release hazardous dust.
- **Thermal Decomposition:** While heat-resistant, it will char and release toxic fumes if exposed to direct flame or extreme temperatures.
