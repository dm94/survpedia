Biogas is a mixture of gases, primarily methane (CH4) and carbon dioxide (CO2), produced by the breakdown of organic matter in the absence of oxygen. It is a renewable energy source that can be produced from local waste.

## Description of what it is like

Biogas is a colorless gas. In its raw form, it often has a slight odor of rotten eggs due to the presence of hydrogen sulfide (H2S), although pure methane and CO2 are odorless. It burns with a blue flame.

## Origin and where to find it

- **Environments**: Swamps, marshes, landfills, and stagnant water bodies.
- **Signs**: Bubbles rising from mud in stagnant water; characteristic "marsh gas" smell.
- **Natural/Synthetic**: Natural, but usually captured through a manufacturing process for use.

### Minimum processing required

- **Capture**: Must be collected in an airtight container to prevent dilution with air.
- **Dewatering**: Removal of water vapor (condensation) to prevent pipe blockage and improve combustion.
- **Scrubbing (Optional)**: Removal of CO2 and H2S to increase energy density and prevent corrosion.

### Tools needed to work on it

- **[Biogas Digester](/en/inventions/biogas-digester)**: For controlled production.
- **[Pipes](/en/inventions/pipes)** and valves: For transport.
- **Gas Storage**: Floating drums or weighted bags to maintain pressure.

### Common forms of use

- **Raw Biogas**: Used directly in stoves and lamps.
- **Biomethane**: Purified biogas, equivalent to natural gas.

### Possible substitutes

- **[Wood Gas](/en/materials/wood-gas)**: Produced from wood thermochemical gasification.
- **[Alcohol](/en/materials/alcohol)**: Liquid fuel for similar applications.
- **Natural Gas**: Fossil counterpart.

### Limitations and common failures

- **Energy Density**: Lower than natural gas due to CO2 content.
- **Pressure**: Requires storage under pressure or in large volumes to be useful.
- **Corrosion**: Raw biogas can corrode metal components if H2S is not removed.

### Risks and safety

- **Asphyxiation**: Can displace oxygen in enclosed spaces.
- **Explosion**: Forms explosive mixtures with air.
- **Toxicity**: H2S is highly toxic even in small concentrations.
