Hydrochloric acid, historically known as *muriatic acid* or *spirit of salt*, is a highly corrosive, strong mineral acid with many industrial uses. It is an aqueous solution of hydrogen chloride gas.

## Description of what it is like

Pure hydrochloric acid is colorless and has a highly pungent, irritating odor. It "fumes" in moist air as the hydrogen chloride gas escapes and reacts with water vapor to form tiny droplets of acid. It is highly corrosive to most metals and organic tissues.

## Origin and where to find it

- **Not natural**: Does not occur naturally in concentrated form on Earth's surface (though it is found in gastric acid).
- **Source**: Must be synthesized from **[Salt](/en/materials/salt)** and a strong acid.

### Minimum processing required

1.  **Salt-Sulfuric Acid Process (Mannheim Process)**:
    - Mix common **[Salt](/en/materials/salt)** (Sodium Chloride) with concentrated **[Sulfuric Acid](/en/materials/sulfuric-acid)**.
    - Heat the mixture in a vessel.
    - Hydrogen chloride gas (HCl) is evolved.
    - The gas is then dissolved in **[Water](/en/materials/water)** to produce hydrochloric acid.
2.  **Synthesis from Elements (Modern)**:
    - Burning **[Hydrogen](/en/elements/hydrogen)** gas in **[Chlorine](/en/elements/chlorine)** gas.

### Tools needed to work on it

- **Acid-resistant vessel**: Glass or high-quality ceramic. It will corrode most metals.
- **Gas absorption system**: A series of containers (Wolff bottles) where the HCl gas can be bubbled through water.
- **Heat source**: To drive the reaction between salt and sulfuric acid.

### Common forms of use

- **Muriatic Acid**: Technical grade, often slightly yellow due to iron impurities, used for masonry cleaning.
- **Pickling Agent**: Used to remove rust and scale from **[Iron](/en/materials/iron)** and **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)** before further processing.
- **Aqua Regia**: Mixed with **[Nitric Acid](/en/materials/nitric-acid)** to dissolve noble metals.

### Possible substitutes

- **[Sulfuric Acid](/en/materials/sulfuric-acid)**: For some cleaning and pH adjustment tasks.
- **[Vinegar](/en/materials/vinegar)**: A much weaker acid for mild cleaning.

### Limitations and common failures

- **Volatilization**: HCl gas constantly escapes from concentrated solutions, especially when heated, reducing its strength.
- **Contamination**: Easily contaminated by metals it comes into contact with, changing its chemical properties.
- **Neutralization**: Reacts quickly with alkaline substances (like **[Lime](/en/materials/lime)**), which neutralizes its acidity.

### Risks and safety

- **Chemical Burns**: Causes severe burns to skin, eyes, and mucous membranes.
- **Inhalation Hazard**: The fumes are highly irritating to the respiratory system and can cause permanent lung damage.
- **Reacts with Bleach**: Mixing hydrochloric acid with **[Bleach](/en/materials/bleach)** produces deadly chlorine gas.

### Related materials

- **[Salt](/en/materials/salt)**: The primary raw material for traditional production.
- **[Sulfuric Acid](/en/materials/sulfuric-acid)**: Required for the displacement reaction.
- **[Nitric Acid](/en/materials/nitric-acid)**: Partner in making aqua regia.
- **[Water](/en/materials/water)**: Necessary solvent to form the liquid acid.
