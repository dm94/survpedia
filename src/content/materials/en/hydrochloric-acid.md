Hydrochloric acid is a strong, colorless, highly corrosive mineral acid with many industrial uses. When dissolved in water, hydrogen chloride gas forms hydrochloric acid.

## Description of what it is like

Pure hydrochloric acid is colorless, but industrial grade often has a yellow tinge due to iron impurities. It has a sharp, irritating, pungent odor. It "fumes" in moist air as the hydrogen chloride gas escapes and reacts with water vapor.

## Origin and where to find it

- **Not natural**: Does not exist freely in nature in significant concentrations (except as stomach acid in animals and in some volcanic gases).
- **Raw Materials**: **[Salt](/en/materials/salt)** (Sodium Chloride) and **[Sulfuric Acid](/en/materials/sulfuric-acid)**.

### Minimum processing required

- **Mannheim Process**:
  1.  Place common **[Salt](/en/materials/salt)** in a furnace.
  2.  Add concentrated **[Sulfuric Acid](/en/materials/sulfuric-acid)**.
  3.  Heat the mixture. A chemical reaction occurs, producing Sodium Sulfate and Hydrogen Chloride gas.
  4.  **Absorption**: Pass the Hydrogen Chloride gas through **[Water](/en/materials/water)**. The gas dissolves readily, creating hydrochloric acid.

### Tools needed to work on it

- **Non-reactive containers**: Glass, ceramic, or certain plastics. It eats through most metals.
- **Distillation/Absorption setup**: To capture the gas and dissolve it in water safely.
- **Ventilation**: Essential to avoid breathing the toxic fumes.

### Common forms of use

- **Muriatic Acid**: An older name for the industrial-grade, slightly impure version.
- **Concentrated**: Usually around 37% HCl in water.

### Possible substitutes

- **Sulfuric Acid**: Stronger, but less volatile; used for different processes.
- **Vinegar (Acetic Acid)**: For very mild cleaning or food use.
- **Citric Acid**: For descaling or food preservation.

### Limitations and common failures

- **Volatiliy**: Unlike sulfuric acid, HCl gas constantly escapes from concentrated solutions.
- **Metal corrosion**: Rapidly destroys iron, steel, and aluminum, releasing flammable hydrogen gas in the process.

### Risks and safety

- **Corrosive**: Causes severe chemical burns to skin and eyes.
- **Toxic Fumes**: Breathing the gas can cause respiratory damage or death.
- **Mixing**: Never mix with bleach (Sodium Hypochlorite), as it releases deadly chlorine gas.
