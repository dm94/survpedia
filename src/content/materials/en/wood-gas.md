Wood gas is a syngas mixture consisting primarily of nitrogen, carbon monoxide, hydrogen, and some methane. It is produced by the partial combustion and reduction of wood or other biomass in a restricted oxygen environment.

## Description of what it is like

Wood gas is a colorless gas. When produced from wood, it may carry a smoky smell if tars are present. It burns with a pale blue or almost invisible flame.

## Origin and where to find it

- **Environments**: Not found in nature. Produced in wood gasifiers.
- **Signs**: Escaping gas from a gasifier or leaking pipes.
- **Natural/Synthetic**: Synthetic (processed).

### Minimum processing required

- **Gasification**: Heating biomass in a restricted-oxygen environment.
- **Cooling**: Must be cooled to increase density and drop out moisture.
- **Filtering**: Removal of soot, ash, and tars is critical for use in engines.

### Tools needed to work on it

- **[Wood Gasifier](/en/inventions/wood-gasifier)**: For production.
- **Coolers/Condensers**: To lower gas temperature.
- **Filters**: Media like cloth, sawdust, or wood chips to capture particulates.

### Common forms of use

- **Engine Fuel**: Replacing gasoline or diesel in adapted engines.
- **Heating Fuel**: Burned in furnaces or boilers.

### Possible substitutes

- **[Biogas](/en/materials/biogas)**: Produced biologically rather than thermochemically.
- **[Alcohol](/en/materials/alcohol)**: Liquid alternative.
- **Coal Gas**: Produced similarly but from coal.

### Limitations and common failures

- **Low Energy Density**: Contains a high percentage of non-flammable nitrogen (from the air used in the process), providing less power than gasoline.
- **Tar Contamination**: Improperly filtered gas will clog engine valves and piston rings.
- **System Inertia**: It takes time for the gasifier to reach operating temperature and produce quality gas.

### Risks and safety

- **CO Poisoning**: Carbon monoxide is a silent killer. Wood gas systems must never be used in enclosed spaces without extreme ventilation and CO detectors.
- **Flashback**: If air enters the gas storage or pipes, a flashback or explosion can occur.
- **Burns**: Gasifiers operate at extreme temperatures.

### Related materials

- **[Wood](/en/materials/wood)**: Primary feedstock.
- **[Charcoal](/en/materials/charcoal)**: Cleaner feedstock with less tar.
- **[Steel](/en/materials/steel)**: Required for construction of the gasifier.
